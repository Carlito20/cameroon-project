## ‚öñÔ∏è Legal & Regulatory Requirements

### Business Registration (Cameroon)
1. **Company Incorporation**
   - Register with Trade and Personal Property Rights Registry
   - Obtain certificate of incorporation

2. **Tax Registration**
   - Obtain Tax Identification Number (TIN)
   - Register for VAT

3. **Chamber of Commerce**
   - Register with local Chamber of Commerce
   - Obtain import license

4. **National Shippers' Council**
   - Register with CNCC (Cameroon National Shippers' Council)
   - Obtain Electronic Cargo Tracking Note (ECTN)

### Import Requirements
1. **Import License**: Required for commercial imports
2. **Pre-Shipment Evaluation (PECAE)**: Certificate of Conformity required
3. **Customs Documentation**:
   - Commercial invoice
   - Bill of lading
   - Packing list
   - Insurance certificate
   - Technical certificates (where applicable)

4. **Customs Duties** (CEMAC Common External Tariff):
   - Category I (Primary necessary goods): 0%
   - Category II (Raw materials/equipment): 10%
   - Category III (Intermediary goods): 20%
   - Category IV (Fast-moving consumer goods): 30%

5. **VAT**: 19.25% on all imported goods

### Compliance
- Ensure all products meet Cameroonian standards
- Proper labeling and documentation
- Expiry date tracking for perishable items
- Storage compliance (temperature, humidity where required)

## üìà Inventory Management

### Tracking System
Each product should track:
- **Brand**: Product manufacturer
- **SKU**: Stock Keeping Unit
- **Expiry Date**: Critical for diapers, food, body care
- **Country of Origin**: USA or Canada
- **Batch/Shipment ID**: For traceability
- **Import Date**: When received in Cameroon
- **Storage Type**: Dry, cool, fragile
- **Unit Type**: Piece, pack, carton
- **Quantity**: Current stock levels
- **Cost**: Purchase and landed cost
- **Selling Price**: Retail price

### Inventory Best Practices
- First-In-First-Out (FIFO) for items with expiry dates
- Regular stock audits
- Reorder point alerts
- Seasonal demand planning
- Dead stock management

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Risk Management

### Key Risks
1. **Import Delays**: Customs clearance issues
   - *Mitigation*: Work with experienced freight forwarders, complete documentation properly

2. **Currency Fluctuation**: Exchange rate changes
   - *Mitigation*: Monitor rates, consider forward contracts

3. **Product Damage**: During shipping
   - *Mitigation*: Proper packaging, insurance coverage

4. **Regulatory Changes**: Import policy changes
   - *Mitigation*: Stay updated on regulations, maintain good relationships with authorities

5. **Inventory Management**: Overstocking or stockouts
   - *Mitigation*: Implement good inventory system, analyze sales data

6. **Competition**: Other importers
   - *Mitigation*: Focus on quality, customer service, unique product selection
